# Loam

Beneath everything that grows, there is ground.

Loam is the ground. Below it lies a substrate — signed, content-addressed, mergeable; a bed of
settled fact that never forgets and never argues. Loam is what lives in that bed: the medium where
scattered fact becomes structure, where structure learns to resolve itself into meaning, and where
meaning, given a little warmth, begins to compute.

It is not a database. A database is a room you put things into and later take them out of. Loam is
closer to soil — a living, self-describing medium whose visible state is only the present tense of a
computation that never stopped running. Its shapes are grown, not imposed. Its rules are written in
the same stuff as its data, so it can rewrite them. Nothing in it is deleted; it only accretes and
reconsiders. Ask it what it knew last spring and it will tell you, exactly — and tell you who said so.

It has no center. Two patches of Loam that meet simply merge, the way two mats of root fuse
underground: no negotiation, no loss, no authority deciding whose truth survives. What is agreed is
held; what is contested is kept in superposition until someone with standing decides. History is
total. Provenance is native. Trust is a lens you hold, not a verdict the ground hands down.

## To consume Loam

You do not build a database and fill it. You declare a shape and a handful of behaviors — what a
thing is, and what should happen when the ground shifts beneath it — and you plant them. The medium
takes them, and from then on it tends them: resolving your shapes, maintaining your views, firing
your behaviors, remembering everything, entirely without you watching.

An app that consumes Loam is not a program sitting atop a store. It is a configuration of a living
medium — a set of intentions entrusted to the soil. Your data does not belong to your app; it
outlives it. Another app may grow beside yours in the same ground, drinking from what you planted,
benefiting from your work without ever learning your name — and you from theirs. They speak only
through the soil. That is the whole architecture.

To consume Loam is to plant something in the ground and walk away, trusting that it keeps growing,
keeps its history, and keeps faith with whoever tends it next.
